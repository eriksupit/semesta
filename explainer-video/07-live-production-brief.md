# Live Actor Production Brief
## Video Explainer Semesta Digital *(working title)*
*Draft v1.0 · 8 Juni 2026*

---

## Overview

This brief covers the live actor / green screen shoot for the Semesta Digital explainer video. It is intended for the director, DOP (Director of Photography), production assistant, and wardrobe coordinator.

**Total live actor footage required:** 90 seconds across 8 segments.

| Segment | Content | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Live actor — opening + first tagline setup | 20 sec |
| S3 | Live actor — transition 1 | 10 sec |
| S5 | Live actor — transition 2 | 10 sec |
| S7 | Live actor — transition 3 | 10 sec |
| S9 | Live actor — transition 4 | 10 sec |
| S11 | Live actor — transition 5 | 10 sec |
| S13 | Live actor — transition 6 (final transition) | 10 sec |
| S17 | Live actor — closing + tagline | 20 sec |

**Recommended shoot:** All 8 segments in one day. Estimated studio time: 4–6 hours including setup, rehearsal, and takes.

## Actor Brief

### Character Profile

The live actor in this video is not playing a character. They are the narrator — but not a standard commercial presenter. Think of them as someone who has *already seen* the ecosystem they are describing. They are not trying to convince the audience. They are simply showing something they know to be real.

**Tone:** Trustworthy. Grounded. Not corporate. Not motivational-speaker. Not news anchor. Closer to: a thoughtful founder speaking one-on-one at a private event.

**What to avoid:**
- Forced enthusiasm
- Sales-voice uplift
- Excessive gesture
- Performative sincerity
- Talking *at* the camera (instead: talking *with* the person on the other side)

### Gender and Appearance

Gender is open — male or female. The role is credibility and warmth, not demographic representation (demographic representation is handled by the AI characters).

- Age range: 35–55 preferred. Old enough to carry authority, young enough to feel contemporary.
- Appearance: Professional but accessible. No power suit. No jeans-and-t-shirt casual. Business casual that reads as "someone you trust, not someone trying to sell you something."
- Ethnicity: Indonesian. Authentically so — not generic "pan-Asian" casting.

### Wardrobe

Single wardrobe for the entire shoot. All 8 segments must match — this signals visual continuity and a single-day shoot in post-production.

**Recommended options:**
- Option A: Dark batik kemeja (modern cut, not traditional full set) + tailored trousers/slacks. Clean and culturally anchored.
- Option B: Smart casual — neutral toned dress shirt (deep earth tone: dark teal, navy, forest green) + tailored trousers. No patterns.

**Avoid:**
- White or very light colors (will bleed on green screen)
- Bright red or green (green-screen spill, red reads aggressive)
- Heavy patterns or logos
- Jewelry that catches light and distracts

**Footwear:** Not visible in planned shots — confirm before finalizing.

## Set and Environment

### Background

**Primary approach: Real location, not green screen composite.**

The live actor background should be a *real, dressed environment* — not a digitally inserted background. This is important for two reasons:
1. Authenticity: real texture reads differently than composited backgrounds, even at high quality.
2. Contrast with AI scenes: live footage will have a tactile warmth that AI scenes, no matter how good, won't fully replicate. This is a feature, not a bug.

**Background character:**
- Not a corporate office
- Not a TV studio backdrop
- Aim for: a workspace that feels personal — exposed brick or textured concrete wall, natural wood element, good window light, minimal. Clean but lived-in.
- If location is unavailable: a well-dressed green screen with a simple warm-toned room environment composited in is acceptable as fallback.

### Lighting Setup

All 8 segments use the same lighting setup. The actor changes position and posture per segment, but lighting stays constant.

**Target:** Warm tungsten, 3200K color temperature. Not daylight (5600K). Not mixed.

**Setup:**
- Key light: Large soft source (octabox or beauty dish) at 45° camera-left or camera-right — actor's choice of which side reads better on camera.
- Fill: Subtle reflector or very soft fill at 50–30% of key. No hard shadows on face.
- Backlight / rim: Light rim to separate actor from background. Warm color — not cool.
- Background: Background light should be slightly warmer than key — 2700K or add a small CTO gel on background lamp. This gives depth and warmth at the same time.
- S17 (closing): Increase key light intensity by ~15–20% from baseline. This segment should feel warmest of all. Don't change setup — just nudge output up.

**What to avoid:**
- Blue-tinted light sources of any kind (LED panels with daylight bias uncorrected)
- Fluorescent background pollution
- Hard shadows on face
- Eye-level flat lighting (feels news anchor / sterile)

### Camera

**Spec:** Any cinema-grade camera capable of 4K ProRes or RAW at 25fps or 24fps. DaVinci Resolve will be used for color grade — ensure footage is in a log or wide color gamut format.

**Frame rate:** 25fps preferred (matches Indonesian broadcast standard and most AI generation output).

**Primary shots per segment:**

| Segment | Shot | Framing | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | Main | Medium shot, slight low angle | Rack focus from BG to face at start |
| S3 | Main | Medium shot, step right from S1 position | Actor moves = video is in motion |
| S5 | Main | Medium shot, actor seated (desk edge or chair) | Postural variation |
| S7 | Main | Medium shot, actor center, standing | Midpoint energy — centered |
| S9 | Main | Medium shot, actor steps closer to camera | Intimacy increases in second half |
| S11 | Main | Medium shot, center | Pace increasing |
| S13 | Main | Medium shot, fully upright, center | Final transition — maximum composure |
| S17 | Main | Medium shot, center, warmest light | Closing — most intimate of all |

For each segment, shoot:
1. Master take (full segment, no cuts)
2. Minimum 2–3 backup takes
3. One close-up take (tighter framing for potential cutaway in edit)

**Do not shoot:** Wide shot (head to toe). Not needed for any segment and wastes time.

## Segment-by-Segment Direction Notes

### S1 — Opening (20 seconds)

Actor enters frame already in position — not walking in. The video begins with them present, in contrast to the faceless cold open that precedes them.

First 3 seconds: **Do not smile yet.** Hold steady eye contact with camera. Let the contrast with S0 breathe for a moment. The warmth of the environment speaks before the actor does.

Smile arrives naturally during second sentence — not performed, organic.

Third sentence ("Tapi bagaimana kalau tidak harus begitu?") is not a rhetorical question — it is an invitation. The vocal delivery should not rise at the end the way a question does. It stays level, or drops very slightly. It is a door being opened, not a challenge being posed.

Final 3 seconds of segment: one small nod, then hold until cut.

### S3 — Transition 1 (10 seconds)

Actor takes one clear step to the right from their S1 position before speaking. This is a physical signal that the video is moving. The step should happen as the previous scene cuts in — it will be visible from the first frame of this segment.

### S5 — Transition 2 (10 seconds)

Actor is now seated. This is the postural variation segment. The seated position communicates: comfortable, accessible, conversational. Not authoritative — warm.

### S7 — Transition 3 (10 seconds)

This is the tonal midpoint of the video. Actor returns to standing, moves to center frame. This transition is deliberately symmetric — sets up the second half.

Three short sentences with deliberate rhythm. Third sentence ("Untuk semua yang mau bermain dengan aturan yang sama") should be delivered slower than the first two. Pause slightly after "aturan yang sama" — let it land before the cut.

### S9 — Transition 4 (10 seconds)

Actor takes one step closer to camera from S7 position. This is a subtle shift — approximately half a step. The audience should feel they are now in a slightly more intimate conversation, even if they can't identify why.

Deliver "lebih besar lagi" with genuine curiosity, not with pitch-man energy. This is not hype. It is as if the actor is also discovering something worth showing.

### S11 — Transition 5 (10 seconds)

Pace of delivery slightly faster than earlier segments — not rushed, but the video is building toward its peak and the actor carries that energy. 

"Seharusnya bisa didengar" — slow this phrase intentionally. These words are the pivot to the final sequence.

### S13 — Transition 6 / Final Transition (10 seconds)

The actor stands fully upright. This is the last moment before the convergence and close — maximum composure.

"Bertemu" — the key word. Deliver it slightly slower than surrounding words. This is the word that signals the video's structure to the audience: everything has been leading here.

### S17 — Closing (20 seconds)

This is the most important segment to get right. Allocate extra takes and time.

The actor should feel, in this segment, that they have just shown the audience something real — and they know the audience already understands what it means. There is no need to convince. The job is invitation.

VO pacing:
- "Satu aturan main. Untuk semua." — Calm. Slightly slow. Each word matters.
- "Puluhan juta kehidupan ekonomi." — Full weight. Not rushed.
- "Satu ekosistem." — Even slower. One breath per sentence.
- [Longest pause in the video — ~0.7 sec] — Hold this. Do not rush into the final line.
- "Dan kamu bisa ada di tengahnya." — This is the only line in the video addressed directly to the viewer as "kamu." Deliver it quietly. Like an invitation into a room, not a CTA. After this line: stillness. A quiet, real smile — not performed. Then hold until fade to black begins.

**On-screen text note for editor:** The English tagline appears at ~second 15 of this segment (superimposed text). Shoot enough clean time after the actor finishes their lines for the text to be visible for a full 5 seconds before fade begins.

## VO Recording

### Session Format

VO recording can happen during the shoot day (preferred) or as a separate session (acceptable).

- If during shoot: actor delivers VO live in sync with camera. This is harder but produces better naturalness.
- If separate: record to scratch track that was already timed against rough video assembly. Ensures sync without re-cutting.

**Strong preference: live delivery.** The naturalness of a single take, in the moment, on set, cannot be fully replicated in a studio session reading to a blank mic.

### Audio Setup

- Microphone: Lavalier (hidden in wardrobe) as primary. Boom mic as backup.
- Room treatment: If shooting in location, ensure HVAC is off during takes. Record 30 seconds of room tone for mixing.
- Target LUFS: -18 LUFS for broadcast delivery. Mix engineer to finalize.

### VO Script Summary (All Segments)

| Seg | VO |
|---|---|
| S1 | "Ada momen ketika semuanya terasa... terlalu rumit. Terlalu banyak pintu. Terlalu banyak aturan yang tidak kamu buat. Tapi bagaimana kalau tidak harus begitu?" |
| S3 | "Dan itu bukan hanya soal satu orang. Ini soal seluruh ekosistem yang bergerak bersama." |
| S5 | "Dari belajar, ke bekerja, ke bertumbuh. Semua di satu tempat." |
| S7 | "Bukan hanya untuk yang kecil. Bukan hanya untuk yang besar. Untuk semua yang mau bermain dengan aturan yang sama." |
| S9 | "Dan kemudian ada yang lebih besar lagi. Bisnis yang sudah terbukti — yang ingin tumbuh lebih jauh." |
| S11 | "Ada yang membawa pengalaman puluhan tahun. Dan pengalaman itu — seharusnya bisa didengar lebih banyak orang." |
| S13 | "Dan kemudian — semua ini bertemu. Dengan yang punya jangkauan paling luas." |
| S17 | "Satu aturan main. Untuk semua. Puluhan juta kehidupan ekonomi. Satu ekosistem. Dan kamu bisa ada di tengahnya." |

**Banned word:** "bayangkan" (imagine). This word must not appear in any take or alternate line. If actor improvises around script, flag any use of this word and do not use those takes.

**Banned in VO:** Names of any religious organizations, government agencies, or competing platforms.

## Post-Production Notes for Live Footage

### Color Grade

- Target: warm, cinematic. Not oversaturated. Not commercial-TV bright.
- Reference palette: golden hour, warm skin tones, earth backdrop. Think cinematic documentary, not commercial product video.
- Ensure S17 reads warmest in the grade — a subtle increase of warmth relative to S1 is correct.
- Do not match live actor footage to AI footage. The live footage should feel slightly more textured and tangible. This difference is intentional.

### Audio in Post

- Sync live actor audio with ambient scoring stem (Phase 2 during character segments, Phase 3 during S17).
- The scoring and live VO should never compete — VO is always intelligible above scoring.
- During live actor segments, scoring drops to approximately 20–30% of its character-segment level. VO is primary.

### On-Screen Text Placement (S17)

The English tagline super in S17 appears at ~second 15 of the segment. Placement: lower third, center-aligned. Same typeface and weight as S16 data segment. White text. No background box or drop shadow. Let the warm background show through — the text needs to be legible but integrated, not imposed.

## Shoot Day Schedule (Suggested)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Crew call. Set up lighting and camera. |
| 9:00 AM | Actor arrives. Wardrobe check and makeup. |
| 9:30 AM | Camera test with actor. Lighting adjustments. |
| 10:00 AM | Rehearsal — read all 8 scripts, run blocking for position changes (S1→S3→S5→S7). |
| 10:30 AM | Shoot S1, S3, S5, S7 (first half — actor moving progressively across positions). |
| 12:00 PM | Lunch break. |
| 1:00 PM | Shoot S9, S11, S13 (second half — tighter camera, building energy). |
| 2:00 PM | Shoot S17 — closing. Allocate most time here. Get multiple takes. |
| 3:30 PM | Coverage and pickups. Additional close-up takes if needed. |
| 4:00 PM | Room tone recording. Wrap. |

## Pre-Shoot Checklist

- [ ] Location confirmed and accessible day before
- [ ] Lighting kit available: large softbox, fill, rim, background lights, CTO gels
- [ ] Camera confirmed: 4K ProRes or RAW capable, 25fps
- [ ] Lens: 50mm or 85mm equivalent (full frame) for medium shots — no wide-angle distortion
- [ ] Audio: lavalier + boom + recorder confirmed
- [ ] Wardrobe finalized and confirmed with actor
- [ ] Script printed for actor reference
- [ ] Continuity: all 8 segment positions marked on floor with tape before actor arrives
- [ ] HVAC turned off during takes confirmed with location
- [ ] Monitor for director to review takes on set
